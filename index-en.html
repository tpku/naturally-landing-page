Trash -
An infinite source?

Did you know that on average every year a person throws away 7.5 kg of textiles every year? This means that the material is not being used as a valuable resource by being reused or recycled. Over 90% of all climate impacts from Swedish textile consumption are currently linked to the purchase of newly produced goods (the percentage calculates the climate impact of both production and consumers' transport to and from the store for the purchase).

In Sweden, purchases of newly produced textiles increased in the early 2000s from 10.7 kilos to around 14 kilos of textiles per person per year. In 2019, each Swede consumed 13.7 kilos of clothing and home textiles. This means that around 141,100 tonnes of clothing and home textiles were placed on the Swedish market (this consumption data only includes imports by companies, not imports by private individuals from other EU countries).

Here at naturally, we are constantly working to achieve a more sustainable environment in the textile industry. We want to reach a point where people act from the heart without having to think about the consequences. We want to help you help everyone. Our collections test the boundaries and we are constantly looking for better and more daring ways to extract controversial materials. For example, we have found the revolutionary way to make a wire from lithium, mercury and copper amalgam that is virtually indestructible and weighs less than 1g per cubic metre. It is the thinnest mass-produced material in the history of the world.

All our resources come originally from nature, which means that it is also at the expense of nature. The only thing we do is to take advantage of all what means rubbish to also make sure that we don't take more than we need.
